Bachelor of Commerce(B.Com.)-Explore Your Path in Commerce

A Bachelor of Commerce (BCom or B Com) is an undergraduate degree in commerce which includes mathematical & law subjects like: accounting, taxation, corporate laws or company law, customs law, business law or mercantile law, auditing, Finance, financial management or corporate finance, banking and insurance, income tax law and practice, goods and services tax, economics, computer applications in business, corporate accounting, cost accounting, financial accounting, higher accounting or advanced accounting, statistics, mathematics or business mathematics, and management-related subjects.

Why Choose B.Com. Commerce?

  • Key Areas in Commerce
  • Accounting and Finance:Recording,analyzing and reporting financial transactions.
  • Business Studies:Understanding how businesses operate and make strategic decision.
  • Economics:Study of resources allocation, markets, an economic system.
  • Corporate Law:Legal framework govering businesses and organizations
  • Marketing:Promoting and selling products/services effectively.

Available Specialization

Accounting & Finance
Accounting and finance courses equip individuals with the skills to manage financial resources, analyze financial data, and make informed business decisions. These courses cover a wide range of topics, including financial accounting, managerial accounting, taxation, auditing, and financial analysis.
Bachelor of Sport Management(BMS)
A Bachelor of Sports Management course provides a foundation in the business and administrative aspects of the sports industry, preparing students for careers in various areas like event management, marketing, and athlete representation. The curriculum typically includes subjects like management, finance, marketing, law, and event planning.
Banking and Insurance(BBI)
A Bachelor of Banking and Insurance (BBI) course is a three-year undergraduate program focused on the principles and practices of the banking and insurance industries. It provides students with a comprehensive understanding of financial concepts, banking operations, insurance products, and related regulatory frameworks.
Business Process Management
A Business Process Management (BPM) course can help individuals and organizations understand and implement effective BPM strategies. It covers topics like process analysis, modeling, improvement, and automation, with the goal of streamlining operations and improving efficiency.
Finance and Management Accounting(BFMA)
Finance and Management Accounting provides a foundation in accounting principles, financial management, and business operations, equipping students with skills to analyze financial data and make informed decisions. This specialized program is a 3-year, full-time undergraduate course that focuses on developing analytical abilities, critical thinking, and ethical business conduct.
