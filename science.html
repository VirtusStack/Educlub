Bachelor of Science(B.Sc.)-Explore Your Path in Science

The Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) program offers a diverse and dynamic foundation in scientific disciplines. Whether you're passionate about technology, biology, chemistry, or data analysis, our B.Sc. Science stream is designed to equip students with theoretical knowledge and practical skills for academic and professional success.

Why Choose B.Sc. Science?

  • Wide range of specialization options
  • Hands-on laboratory and project-based learning
  • Experienced faculty and modern facilities
  • Strong foundation for higher studies or research

Available Specialization

Aviation
Aviation courses encompass a wide range of programs designed to train individuals for various roles within the aviation industry. These courses cover areas like pilot training, aircraft maintenance, aviation management, and air traffic control.
Biotechnology
A biotechnology course combines biology and technology to create new products and processes in areas like medicine, food production, and environmental protection. It involves studying subjects like molecular biology, genetics, and microbiology, along with applying these principles to develop new technologies and products.
Botany
Botany courses cover the study of plants and their diverse aspects. They include subjects like plant anatomy, physiology, genetics, and ecology. These courses can be pursued at undergraduate (BSc) and postgraduate (MSc, MPhil, PhD) levels, as well as through online platforms and short courses.
Chemistry
A Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Chemistry typically covers core areas like Inorganic, Organic, and Physical Chemistry, along with Analytical Chemistry. It often includes specialized electives and may offer concentrations in areas like Biochemistry or Forensic Chemistry.
Computer Science
A BSc in Computer Science is a 3-year undergraduate degree that provides a broad foundation in computer science concepts, programming, and software development. It's a good alternative to a BTech in Computer Science, offering similar career prospects but potentially saving a year of study. The curriculum typically covers programming languages, data structures, algorithms, computer architecture, and other foundational topics.
Information Technology
A BSc (IT) is a 3-year undergraduate degree focused on Information Technology, encompassing software development, software testing, web design, databases, programming, and computer networking. It prepares students for careers in various IT fields, such as software engineering, network administration, and database management.
